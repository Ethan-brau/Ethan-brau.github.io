The history of the Volvo 200 series

______________________

It's the Autumn of 1974

and automotive company, Volvo just wrapped up their prototype for the new Volvo 200 series, spending years refining their 2.3L Volvo B23, one of their most robust engines yet. Boasting just about 110 horsepower naturally aspirated and somewhere in the ballpark of 135 horsepower with the turbocharged option. The Iconic style of the volvo 240's and 200 series in general have brought this style in the 80's and 90's in automotive history, this style was called; "Turbo Bricks". The release of this new Volvo car in the 70s brought a new age of fun, ease of maintanence, spectacular Swedish engineering and practicality while remaining affordable.

_____________________

_____________________

Volvo released many ad campaigns, displaying the new 240's balanced power, comfort, and above all else, the sheer robustness, durability and safety of their new cars. Volvo has been #1 in safety worldwide for many of years, they invented the 3 point seatbelt and didn't patent the design intentionally, so more automotive companies would take up their safe practices. Starting with the volvo 240's, they sold their cars with the option of an ABS (anti-lock brake system) with the innovative DOT 4 brake fluid to increase thermal resistance for the brake system, mandatory airbags for collisions, negative space in the cabin so passengers aren't crushed during a collision, interion impact bars and roll cage in the chassis of the vehicle. All of these features displaying the intensity of Volvo's safety standards throughout the 80's.
